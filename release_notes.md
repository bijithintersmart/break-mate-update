## BreakMate v1.2.0 — Contextual Intelligence & Personal Wellness

This update introduces the Contextual Wellness Engine. We've designed this to move BreakMate beyond a simple timer and into an intelligent partner that understands your work environment and helps you maintain healthy habits throughout your day.

### Contextual Intelligence

- Contextual Wellness Engine:
  BreakMate now identifies which application you are currently using and offers specific wellness advice based on that activity.
  - Coding: We focus on posture checks to help prevent the common "coder's hunch."
  - Writing: We prioritize wrist relief and forearm stretches.
  - Browsing: We include reminders for the 20-20-20 rule to reduce eye strain.
  - Meetings: We suggest a quick mental reset after you finish a long call.

- Calendar-Aware Planning:
  By connecting to your system calendar, BreakMate can now detect upcoming meetings. The app will automatically postpone scheduled breaks if you are in a meeting or if one is starting soon, so your deep work remains uninterrupted.

- Smart End-of-Day Wind-Down:
  We've added a wind-down session that triggers at your set clock-out time. This includes a summary of your day, showing your total focus time, breaks completed, and overall wellness efficiency.

### Personalization & Goals

- Personal Goals Section:
  You can now set your own daily targets for focus hours and break counts. You can also configure a sitting limit to be notified when you've been stationary for too long, helping you stay active.

- Wellness Library:
  A new dedicated page lets you browse all available wellness activities. You can favorite the exercises you find most helpful or hide the ones that don't work for your current desk setup.

- Companion Progression:
  We've updated how you unlock new pets and themes. Instead of relying on strict daily streaks which can feel like unnecessary pressure, progression is now based on consistency days. Any day you complete at least one break counts toward your progress.

### Privacy & Transparency

- Local-Only Privacy Report:
  A new section in your settings details exactly what information the app uses and how it is managed. All your tracking and calendar data is stored locally on your machine using ObjectBox; nothing ever leaves your computer.

### Technical Improvements

- Dependency Update: We have migrated to the device_calendar_plus library for better stability on macOS and Windows, and we've resolved internal version conflicts.
- UI Refinements: We updated several interface elements to use more modern standards and improved the overall consistency of the design.
- Reliability: We corrected the logic for tracking streaks and improved how settings are saved to ensure your preferences are always preserved.

### Installation Note (macOS)

Because BreakMate is developed independently, macOS may show a security warning when you first launch it.

1. Drag BreakMate to your Applications folder from the installer.
2. Right-click the BreakMate app and select Open.
3. If it is still blocked, you can go to System Settings > Privacy & Security and click "Allow Anyway."
